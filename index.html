Abstract

The ability to perform causal and counterfactual reasoning are central properties of human intelligence. Machines that can perform these types of reasoning are desirable as they could help make decision-making systems more generalizable and interpretable. Simulations have helped advance the state-of-the-art in this domain, by providing the ability to systematically vary parameters (e.g., confounders, both hidden and measurable) and generate examples of the outcomes in the case of counterfactual scenarios. We present a high-fidelity simulation environment that is designed for experiments on causal discovery and counterfactual reasoning. We use the safety-critical real-world application of driving/vehicle navigation. and perform systematic experiments to illustrate the affordances of the this environment, highlighting the challenges and opportunities.
